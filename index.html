I'm Jade Chang, let's create!

I like to make things. In the last year I discovered, I dont even need a pen or paints. I was writing a story when I had an idea. what if the very things I dreamt for my characters could be more than fiction? what if with the right tools I could make them a reality? So here I am, trying to answer my own question by new developing skills.

2015

18 years old. Avid reader and daydreamer with a passion for writing flash fiction. I was a strong believer in live it to write it.

2016

19,I kept writing and started to pursue a career in events and hospitality. grabbing opportunites to try and experience life. I took a foundation year in psychology at Goldsmiths, to better understand how the mind works

2017

20, I moved out of my parents home and began working fulltime, my creativity blossomed. I began painting and became entrenched in writing a new kind of narrative. My obsession with science fiction and dystopias greatly influenced my work during this time. However I found myself truly enjoying hospitality, I switched into bars and pubs in hopes of greater opportunites and a schedule that would allow me to nurture my natural talents.

2018

21, I took a break. It was time to breathe. I had undergone several massive life changes and I felt it in my best interest to take a moment, to heal, reflect and get ready for the sprint ahead.

2019

This year was truly fast paced. I joined a pub group at the end of 2018 and worked header to work my way up and prove myself to be a valuable asset. Due to this, I recieved two promotions and pay rises. I was hungry to learn more and do more, I felt unstoppable.

2020

The year that forced me to stop. During the pandemic, I got back in touch with myself and my creativity. I realised I wanted something new and I started to think about what that may be. I started writing again, a story set in a near future. It featured an app, fully integrated into daily life, mobilised with micro-tech. I thought to myself, If I could write it, why not make it? I approached a friend and we got to work. Eventually I grew frustrated that I could not write or understand code myself. It was time to learn, I aimed to become literate in javascript within the next year!

2021

After some solo study, I applied for and landed my place on the Black CodherTech course!
